Yawkey Robotics Challenges


Hey there! This is the projects homepage for the Yawkey Roxbury Robitics site this semester. This page will host explanations to every week's challenge as well as offering possible solutions for completed challenges.

You can find the complete schedule over at the Roxbury Robotics curriculum page.

After finishing your base robot, be sure to attach the necessary sensors with the sensor building instructions.

Weekly feedback form here (submit photos of your robots here too).

Week 1

Movement and Loops

This week is about making your robots move. We'll be covering basic movement with the wheel motors and later get your robot moving using loops!

Challenges:
  • Get your robot to move forward permanently!
  • Have your robot spin in a circle 3 times!
  • Make your robot spin left two times and then right two times!
    • March 1st

    Week 2

    If-Blocks and Touch Sensors

    This week we'll be working with the touch sensors on your robots! Our goal for this week will to show off the usage of your sensors as well as learn what the If Blocks can do.

    Challenges:
  • With the sensor on the front, let your robot run into a wall and use the sensor to know when to stop.
  • Using a similar If block, make the robot spin around and go back the way it came when it hits the wall.
  • Program the robot to stop when the button is pressed, but keep moving forward when released.
    • March 8th

    Week 3

    Distance Sensors

    This week we will continue using the switch blocks with a new sensor! Our goal this week is to show off using multiple switches with our robot. We'll start thinking about our overall goal with creating a "GuardBot" and what each sensor will be used for.

    Challenges:
  • Using the distance sensor, make your robot keep moving forward if there's nothing in front of it.
  • Program your robot to wave it's arms if something moves too close to it.
  • Make your robot move at a slow speed, but if something gets too close, the robot will charge ahead and move its arms.
  • EXTRA- Use the touch sensor as a toggle button, switching the robot between a 'fast' mode and a 'slow' mode. A "toggle button" in this sense means you should not hold down the button between modes.
    • March 15th

    Week 4

    Color Sensors

    This week we will start using the color sensor. The color sensor will be extremely important for letting your robot know where it is and where it should be going. For our "GuardBot" project, we will be using the color sensor during the robots Patrol mode, keeping it inside a spefic region while on patrol.

    Challenges:
  • With your robot on a desk or table, have your robot move slowly forward and start to reverse once it detects the edge.
  • Have your robot move fast normally but slow down once it detects a certain color beneath it.
  • EXTRA - With your robot on a desk or table, have your robot move slowly forward normally. Once the robot detects an edge, it should spin itself around. The robot should keep spinning until it detects the table/desk again and continue moving forward safely.
    • March 22nd

    Week 5

    Switches

    This week we'll be using our other touch sensor as a switch to change our robots functions at the press of a button! This switch is how we're going to change our GuardBots from Guard mode and Recon mode. Since making a switch is a bit complicated, here's an example down below.

    Here we can see there are two loops inside our main program loop. Each loops has a question: was the button pressed. If the answer is no, the a color will be displayed. If the answer is yes, we use the loop interrupt to move onto the next question. We can use this system of loop interrupt blocks to have multiple questions lined up next to each other.


    Challenges:
  • Give your robot two modes: a fast mode and a slow mode. Use the switch the switch between the two.
  • Have your robot's light switch between the three color modes. Each button press should keep the color lit until the button is pressed again and the color changes.
  • EXTRA - Give your robot the following two modes. Mode 1: Green light when an object is in front of the distance sensors and red when there is no object detected. Mode 2: Red light when an object is in front of the distance sensors and green when there is no object detected.
    • March 29th
